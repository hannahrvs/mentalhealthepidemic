The Mental Health Epidemic
Exploreabout

Since the COVID-19 pandemic in late 2019 and early 2020, the ways of living for everyone around the world shifted drastically. Being required to isolate indoors, school and work turning from in-person to online created a lonely world for everyone. Explore data visualizations to track trends in mental health disorders from multiple data sets, which include information from surveys, questionnaires, text conversations, and popular internet searches, covering both pre and post-COVID-19 periods.

Student Mental Health in Malaysia 2020

Malaysia is one of the "leading countries for higher education" in Asia (Kotera, Ting, & Neary, 2021). This could mean increased pressure on students due to the standards of the higher education. To analyze the the mental health epidemic on Malaysian students, we look at this data set from a survey taken from The International Islamic University of Malaysia (IIUM).

COVID-19 Mental Health Search Terms

This data set showcases the trends of certain search terms on Google following the outbreak of the COVID-19 pandemic. By outlining the trends of these search terms, we can see the concerns of the public and its impact to the mental health of people around the world. The search terms that were measured were "mental health", "depression", "anxiety", "ocd", "obsessive compulsive disorder", "insomnia", "panic attack", "counseling", "psychiatrist".

Mental Health Corpus

The Mental Health Corpus (Namdari, 2023) is an extensive collection of writings covering anxiety, depression, and other mental health issues. Each writing is classified as harmful or non-harmful. Wordclouds were created to represent the most common words that were brought up in the corpus to analyze the language and sentiment surrounding mental health issues.

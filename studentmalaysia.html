Student Mental Health in Malaysia 2020

International Islamic University of Malaysia

Malaysia is one of the "leading countries for higher education" in Asia (Kotera, Ting, & Neary, 2021). This could mean increased pressure on students due to the standards of the higher education. To analyze the the mental health epidemic on malaysian students, we look at this data set from a survey taken from The International Islamic University of Malaysia (IIUM). IIUM is one of the top public universities in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia founded in 1983. To get accepted into this this university, students are required to have score of 550 in the Test of English as a Foreign Language (TOEFL) or 6.0 points in International English Language Testing System (IELTS).

Distribution of Depression, Anxiety, and Panic Attacks in IIUM

In the survey taken by 100 students in August 2020, they were asked to report on their mental health status, including if they have depression, anxiety, or if they experience panic attacks. According to the data, 35 students reported on having depression, 34 reported having anxiety, and 33 experiencing panic attacks.

Looking further into the data, we discover that at least two-thirds of the students who took the survey reported having at least one mental health disorder: depression, anxiety, or panic attacks.

The cumulative grade point average (CGPA) is a mechanism to evaluate a university graduate's knowledge in Malaysia, similar to the GPA system in US college systems. Here, CGPA is measured on a 4.00 scale as well, with higher CGPA meaning a higher level of academic achievement and proficiency in one's courses.

In Figure 3, CGPA was graphed against the number of people who have a mental health disorder present. We see that the majority of the surveyed college students at IIUM have a CGPA above a 3.00 but also have a mental health disorder present. Looking across all the CGPA groups, there was at least one participant with a mental health disorder present.

COVID-19 Mental Health Search Terms

This data set showcases the trends of certain search terms on Google following the outbreak of the COVID-19 pandemic. By outlining the trends of these search terms, we can see the concerns of the public and its impact to the mental health of people around the world. The search terms that were measured were "mental health", "depression", "anxiety", "ocd", "obsessive compulsive disorder", "insomnia", "panic attack", "counseling", "psychiatrist". The search interest was scaled with a number between 0 and 100, where 100 is the most popular point of the week, 1 meaning the least interest, and 0 means no data. The data was collected through Google Trends.

Google Search Trends of Mental Health Disorders Across the World

In figure 1, the search interest rated from 0 to 100 are graphed for the search terms of 'depression', 'anxiety', 'insomnia', 'panic attack', and 'mental health'. In general, we see the search terms 'depression', 'anxiety', 'insomnia', and 'panic attack' score higher on the search interest than the 'mental health' search term. We see that week 4 of December experienced a drop in all the search interest, perhaps attributed to the rising concern of COVID-19 in mid to late December of 2019.

Google Search Trends of 'Psychiatrist' or 'Counseling' Across the World

Figure 2 shows the search interest of the words 'Psychiatrist' and 'Counseling'. The trend shows that same sharp decrease of search interest as Figure 1 in December 2019. There is then a sharp increase shortly the next week and then stays constant for the next couple of weeks. In mid-March 2020, which is the time that COVID-19 cases rapidly started popping up across the world, we see that the search interest for 'counseling' and 'psychiatrist' sharply decreased as well. Perhaps, this is also attributed to the rising concern of COVID-19 around the world. Over time, we see that the search interest starts to steadily increase as the isolation period went on.

Google Search Trends of 'Panic Attack' Across the World

In figure 3, there is a noticible increase in the search interest of the words 'panic attack' from mid-February up until the beginning of April. COVID-19 first hit the US in January 2020, which could attribute to the rising search interest of 'panic attacks' as people could be experiencing more panic attacks because of the upcoming pandemic.

Google Search Trends of 'Insomnia' Across the World

Figure 4 shows a strong positive trend of the search interest of 'Insomnia' during mid-March, the time where the lockdown begun. The next few weeks show large increments of search interest, which could possibly be attributed to the sudden shift from in-person activities to isolation.
